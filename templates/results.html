How does the population of a county affect the amount of tested and positive inidividuals?

This plot is a response to the perceived lack of testing availability despite the widespread rate of infection. To analyze how well counties were doing as far as diagnosing and treating citizens, rate of deaths, infection, and testing were all compared to the population of a county. The basic trend is that as population increases, percentage of population tested decreases. The same trend can be seen in positive cases and deaths, as is to be expected because the less people are tested, the less people will be tested positive. Milwaukee, an outlier in population (nearly 1 million) so not included on the plot, has the highest infection rate, with 0.25% of the population testing positive and has had 1.34% of the population tested. Compared to Eau Claire, a much smaller population of 100,000 has had 0.2% more of their population tested and 0.23% less positive cases. This difference suggests that there is a benefit to widespread testing regardless of symptoms. Testing even those that do not appear ill will be able to give proper care and quarantine to all, overall reducing the number of positive cases.
